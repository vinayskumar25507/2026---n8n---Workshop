⚡ What is No-Code Automation?

Traditionally, if you wanted computers to talk to each other, you needed to spend years learning complex coding languages. No-code platforms like n8n change this entirely.

They provide a visual canvas where you drag, drop, and connect "nodes" that represent logic. It is like building with LEGO bricks for software—you focus on the logic of the workflow, and the platform handles the underlying technical communication.

🧩 The Node Cheat Sheet

Keep this reference guide handy while building your workflow.

Node Name The Analogy What It Actually Does
Schedule Trigger The Alarm Clock ⏰ Starts your automation automatically at a specific time every day.
RSS Read The Newsboy 📰 Fetches the latest published articles from a specific website.
HTTP Request The Digital Waiter 🤵 Communicates with external APIs (like SerpAPI) to fetch live, real-time data from the entire internet.
Merge The Organizer 📁 Combines separate data streams (like RSS and APIs) into one workflow path.
Aggregate The Stapler 🖇️ Bundles separate items into one single JSON document so the AI can read it all at once.
Gemini AI The Brain 🧠 Reads your aggregated data, applies your prompt, and writes the clean summary.
Gmail Send The Delivery Person 📬 Takes the AI's final text and emails it directly to your inbox.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Where do I find my SerpAPI key?

Log in to your SerpAPI account, navigate to the Dashboard, and click on "API Key" in the left-hand menu. Copy that string and paste it into the api_key parameter in your HTTP node.

Why isn't my Gmail connecting?

Ensure you have clicked "Create New Credential" in the Gmail Send node and used the direct "Sign in with Google" button. You may need to click "Advanced" and "Go to [unsafe]" to bypass Google's developer warning during setup.

Can I change the Gemini prompt?

Absolutely! The prompt is entirely customizable. You can ask Gemini to summarize the news into bullet points, translate it into another language, or even write it in the style of a pirate. Just edit the text inside the Basic LLM Chain Node.
